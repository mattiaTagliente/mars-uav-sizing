SECTION 1: HOVER POWER FROM MOMENTUM THEORY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SOURCE: Leishman "Principles of Helicopter Aerodynamics" (Chapter 2)
Check Zotero: @leishmanPrinciplesHelicopterAerodynamics2006

1.1 ACTUATOR DISK MODEL

Present the idealized actuator disk model with assumptions:
- Uniform flow through disk
- No swirl in wake
- Ideal fluid (inviscid)

"The actuator disk model treats the rotor as an infinitely thin disk that
imparts momentum to the air passing through it [@leishmanPrinciplesHelicopterAerodynamics2006]."

1.2 INDUCED VELOCITY

Derive the induced velocity in hover:

Conservation of momentum:
T = ṁ × ΔV = ρ × A × v_i × 2v_i = 2ρAv_i²

Solving for induced velocity:
$$v_i = \sqrt{\frac{T}{2\rho A}}$$ {#eq:induced-velocity}

Where:
- T = thrust (N)
- ρ = air density (kg/m³)
- A = rotor disk area (m²)
- v_i = induced velocity (m/s)

CITE: Leishman Chapter 2, Equation 2.XX

1.3 IDEAL HOVER POWER

The ideal power is thrust times induced velocity:
$$P_{ideal} = T \times v_i = T \times \sqrt{\frac{T}{2\rho A}}$$ {#eq:ideal-power-1}

Rearranging:
$$P_{ideal} = \frac{T^{3/2}}{\sqrt{2\rho A}}$$ {#eq:ideal-power-2}

Or in terms of disk loading (DL = T/A):
$$P_{ideal} = T \times \sqrt{\frac{DL}{2\rho}}$$ {#eq:ideal-power-3}

CITE: Leishman Chapter 2, Equation 2.XX

1.4 FIGURE OF MERIT

The Figure of Merit (FM) accounts for non-ideal effects:
- Tip losses
- Profile drag
- Non-uniform inflow

Definition:
$$FM = \frac{P_{ideal}}{P_{actual}} = \frac{P_{ideal}}{P_{hover}}$$ {#eq:figure-of-merit}

Typical values:
- Large helicopters (high Re): FM = 0.75-0.80
- Small rotors (moderate Re): FM = 0.60-0.70
- Mars conditions (low Re): FM = 0.55-0.65

CITE: Leishman for definition
CITE: Johnson MSH paper for Mars FM values (@johnsonMarsScienceHelicopter2020)

1.5 ACTUAL HOVER POWER

From FM definition:
$$P_{hover} = \frac{P_{ideal}}{FM} = \frac{T^{3/2}}{FM \times \sqrt{2\rho A}}$$ {#eq:hover-power}

For hover, T = W (weight):
$$P_{hover} = \frac{W^{3/2}}{FM \times \sqrt{2\rho A}}$$ {#eq:hover-power-weight}

IMPORTANT: Note the W^(3/2) dependency - hover power scales strongly with weight.

1.6 DISK LOADING FORMULATION

Express hover power in terms of disk loading:
$$P_{hover} = W \times \sqrt{\frac{W/A}{2\rho}} \times \frac{1}{FM}$$

$$P_{hover} = W \times \sqrt{\frac{DL}{2\rho}} \times \frac{1}{FM}$$ {#eq:hover-power-dl}

Where DL = W/A = disk loading (N/m²)

The power loading (P/W) for hover is:
$$\frac{P}{W} = \frac{1}{FM} \times \sqrt{\frac{DL}{2\rho}}$$ {#eq:hover-power-loading}

This is the HOVER CONSTRAINT on the matching chart.

SECTION 3: FORWARD FLIGHT POWER
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

For rotorcraft in forward flight, power requirements include:
- Induced power (reduced from hover due to forward speed)
- Profile power (blade drag)
- Parasite power (fuselage drag)

SOURCE: Leishman Chapter 5 or Prouty "Helicopter Aerodynamics"

3.1 FORWARD FLIGHT POWER COMPONENTS

Total power in forward flight:
$$P_{total} = P_{induced} + P_{profile} + P_{parasite}$$ {#eq:forward-power}

For simplified analysis, use equivalent lift-to-drag ratio concept:

$$\left(\frac{L}{D}\right)_{eff} = \frac{W \times V}{P_{total}}$$ {#eq:equivalent-ld}

3.2 ROTORCRAFT EQUIVALENT L/D

Research typical equivalent L/D for rotorcraft:

| Aircraft Type | (L/D)_eff | Source |
|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Conventional helicopter | 4-6 | Leishman |
| Optimized compound | 6-8 | Literature |
| Pure multirotor | 3-5 | Low, no translational lift |

For Mars rotorcraft estimate: (L/D)_eff ≈ 4

CITE: Find source for rotorcraft L/D values in Leishman or Johnson

3.3 FORWARD FLIGHT POWER

From equivalent L/D:
$$P_{forward} = \frac{W \times V}{(L/D)_{eff}}$$ {#eq:forward-power-ld}

Electrical power:
$$P_{electric,forward} = \frac{W \times V}{(L/D)_{eff} \times \eta_{motor} \times \eta_{ESC}}$$ {#eq:forward-electric}

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: BATTERY ENERGY AND ENDURANCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 AVAILABLE BATTERY ENERGY

The usable battery energy is:
$$E_{available} = m_{batt} \times e_{spec} \times DoD \times \eta_{discharge}$$ {#eq:battery-energy}

Where:
- m_batt = battery mass (kg)
- e_spec = specific energy (Wh/kg), typically 200-250 for Li-ion
- DoD = depth of discharge (0.80-0.90)
- η_discharge = discharge efficiency (~0.95)

Using battery mass fraction:
$$E_{available} = f_{batt} \times MTOW \times e_{spec} \times DoD \times \eta$$ {#eq:battery-energy-fraction}

VALUES (from prompts 02C, 02E):
- f_batt = 0.35 (from mass fractions analysis)
- e_spec = 250 Wh/kg (LiPo, optimistic)
- DoD = 0.80
- η = 0.95

Combined: E_available = 0.35 × MTOW × 250 × 0.80 × 0.95 = 66.5 × MTOW Wh

For MTOW = 4 kg: E_available = 266 Wh

4.2 HOVER ENERGY CONSUMPTION

Energy consumed during hover:
$$E_{hover} = P_{electric,hover} \times t_{hover}$$ {#eq:hover-energy}

4.3 FORWARD FLIGHT ENERGY

For rotorcraft traversing distance D at velocity V:
$$E_{forward} = P_{electric,forward} \times \frac{D}{V} = \frac{W \times D}{(L/D)_{eff} \times \eta}$$ {#eq:forward-energy}

4.4 TOTAL MISSION ENERGY

$$E_{mission} = E_{hover} + E_{forward} + E_{reserve}$$ {#eq:mission-energy}

Where E_reserve = 0.20 × (E_hover + E_forward) for 20% reserve

4.5 ENDURANCE CALCULATION

For pure rotorcraft mission (all forward flight):

$$t_{endurance} = \frac{E_{available}}{P_{electric,forward}}$$ {#eq:endurance}

Substituting:
$$t_{endurance} = \frac{f_{batt} \times MTOW \times e_{spec} \times DoD \times \eta_{batt}}{W \times V / ((L/D)_{eff} \times \eta_{prop})}$$

Simplifying (W = MTOW × g):
$$t_{endurance} = \frac{f_{batt} \times e_{spec} \times DoD \times \eta_{batt} \times (L/D)_{eff} \times \eta_{prop}}{g \times V}$$ {#eq:endurance-simple}

THIS IS THE KEY EQUATION: Endurance depends on battery fraction, specific
energy, and equivalent L/D, but is INDEPENDENT of MTOW (for given fractions).
